## 📘 Module 1: What is a Gem? — With vs Without + Demo Gallery (40 min)

### 1.1 The Problem Gems Solve (5 min)

> Every time you use Gemini, you brief it from scratch. A Gem is a colleague you **onboard once** — it remembers the role, tone, rules, and context forever.

- **Analogy:** Hiring a specialist vs. using a temp every day
- The "Blank Page Problem" — most people stare at the prompt box not knowing where to start
- Gems eliminate this by pre-loading **P**ersona + Task + Context + Format

### 1.2 Live Comparison: With vs Without a Gem (10 min)

**The moment of truth.** Show the identical task done both ways:

**Without a Gem** — Open plain Gemini, attach a document, type:

> "Create a report from this document."
> → Show the result: generic, wrong tone, missing structure, not what you need.

**With a Gem** — Open the "Report Generator" Gem, attach the same document, type:

> "Create a report with the attached doc."
> → Show the result: perfect format, correct tone, executive summary, action items table, Vopak branding.

**Why?** The Gem already has the **Persona** ("You are a Vopak business analyst"), the **Task** ("Always produce a structured report"), the **Context** (grounding files), and the **Format** ("Use markdown tables, max 1 page"). The user only needs to provide the data.

### 1.3 Anatomy of a Great Gem (5 min)

| Component                 | What it does                                       | Example                                                                                                  |
| :------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Name**                  | Identity                                           | "Contract Red-Flag Scanner"                                                                              |
| **System Instructions**   | The hardcoded brain — persona, rules, tone, format | "You are a senior contract analyst at Vopak. Flag payment terms > 30 days. Respond in a markdown table." |
| **Knowledge Base**        | Grounding files — PDFs, Docs, Sheets               | 2026 Pricing Matrix, Standard T&Cs                                                                       |
| **Conversation Starters** | Quick-launch prompts                               | "Analyze this contract for red flags"                                                                    |

### 1.4 Gem Demo Gallery (20 min)

Facilitator demos 4 pre-built Gems, starting simple and building in complexity:

#### Demo 1: Tone Translator Gem (3 min)

- **Name:** "Vopak Tone Translator"
- **Instructions:** "Rewrite any text to reflect Vopak's values: Trustworthy, Collaborative, Courageous. Keep it concise. Never use passive voice."
- **Test:** Paste a blunt email → show the transformed professional output
- **Key learning:** 2 minutes to build, saves 10 minutes per email

#### Demo 2: Meeting Summary Machine (5 min)

- **Name:** "Meeting Tracker"
- **Instructions:** "Summarize raw meeting notes into: Purpose (1 sentence), Key Decisions (list), Action Items (table: Owner | Action | Deadline), Open Questions."
- **Test:** Paste messy meeting notes → show structured output
- **Key learning:** Never write meeting minutes manually again

#### Demo 3: Email Draft Assistant (5 min)

- **Name:** "Professional Email Drafter"
- **Instructions:** "Draft professional emails based on bullet points I give you. Use Vopak tone. Include a subject line suggestion. Sign off with 'Kind regards.'"
- **Test:** Give 3 bullet points → show a polished email
- **Key learning:** From brain dump to professional email in seconds

#### Demo 4: Contract Red-Flag Scanner (7 min)

- **Name:** "Contract Red-Flag Scanner" (grounded with T&Cs document)
- **Instructions:** "Analyze contracts against Vopak Standard T&Cs. Flag deviations in a table: Clause | Our Standard | Contract Terms | Risk Level."
- **Test:** Paste a contract excerpt → show flagged risks with exact T&C citations
- **Key learning:** Grounding makes the Gem factual — it cites your actual documents

## 📘 Module 2: Advanced Gems (30 min)

### 2.1 Grounding with Knowledge (10 min)

Attach files so your Gem **never hallucinates** about company data.

**The limits of direct file upload:**

- You can upload up to 10 files directly to a Gem.
- Great for: a specific policy, a pricing matrix, a single SOP.

**The "Super Grounding" method (NotebookLM Integration):**
What if your Gem needs to know the entire department's history (up to 300 sources)?

1. Create a Notebook in [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google.com).
2. Upload up to 300 sources (PDFs, Docs, websites, audio).
3. Copy the URL of your NotebookLM notebook.
4. Paste that URL into a Google Doc → save as "Department Knowledge Base Source."
5. Upload _that single Google Doc_ to your Gem's Knowledge Base.
6. Your Gem can now reason across all 300 sources, with citations.

**Demo:** Show the "Contract Red-Flag Scanner" citing exact clauses from a library of Vopak T&Cs.

### 2.2 Sharing & Team Gems (10 min)

- Share a Gem via link — team members see it instantly in their own Gem Manager
- **Best practice:** 3–5 shared Gems per team, covering the most repetitive tasks
- **Governance:** Team lead reviews Gem Instructions before wide distribution to ensure Zero Trust compliance

### 2.3 The Gem Architect (10 min)

A meta-Gem that writes System Instructions for other Gems.

**Demo prompt:** "Help me create a Gem for a Procurement Manager who compares vendor quotes. Extract pricing, delivery timelines, and warranty terms from uploaded PDFs into a comparison table."

> **Key insight:** If you feel stuck writing instructions, ask Gemini to help. The Gem Architect is one of the most powerful onboarding tools you have.

## 📘 Module 3: Workspace Studio — What It Is, How It Works & Live Demo (30 min)

> **Key message:** "Gems are your AI colleagues — they think on demand when you ask them. Studio is where your Gems go on autopilot. Describe what you want automated in plain language, and Studio builds a flow that runs in the background — without you touching it."

**What Studio does:**

- Automate everyday work — from simple tasks to complex processes, **no coding required**
- Build AI-powered flows that monitor, respond, and process on your behalf
- Pre-built connectors for Gmail, Chat, Drive, Calendar, and third-party apps
- Templates gallery with dozens of ready-to-use automations

**The anatomy of a Studio flow — 3 building blocks:**

| Building Block | What it does                                      | Examples                                                                |
| :------------- | :------------------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Starter**    | The event or schedule that triggers your flow     | "When I get an email from…" / "Every Monday at 9 AM"                    |
| **Steps**      | The actions the flow performs after it starts     | Ask Gemini to extract action items, draft a reply, add content to a doc |
| **Variables**  | Dynamic placeholders that pass data between steps | Gemini's response, the sender's email address, a form response          |

**Key concept:** Each step produces variables that later steps can use — that's how flows chain actions together automatically.

**Three ways to create a flow:**

| Method             | How                                           | Best for                |
| :----------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :---------------------- |
| **Create with AI** | Describe in plain language → Gemini builds it | Beginners (recommended) |
| **From Template**  | Browse the Discover page → customize          | Common use cases        |
| **Build Manually** | New flow → pick Starter → add Steps           | Power users             |

### Live Demo 1: Email Triage Flow (10 min)

1. Open [studio.workspace.google.com](https://studio.workspace.google.com)
2. In the **"Describe a task for Gemini"** box, type:

   > "When I receive an email from a customer with the word 'complaint' or 'urgent', draft a professional acknowledgment response, classify it as High Priority, and send a summary to the team Chat space."

3. Show the generated flow: **Starter** (email received) → **Step 1** (Gemini classifies) → **Step 2** (draft reply) → **Step 3** (post to Chat)
4. Click **Test run** → show the result live
5. Click **Turn on** → it's now running 24/7

**Good prompt:** "Notify me in Google Chat whenever I get an email from person@example.com"
**Bad prompt:** "Handle emails from person@example.com" ← too vague — always be specific!

### What Studio Can and Can't Do (5 min)

| ✅ Studio CAN                              | ❌ Studio CAN'T (yet)                   |
| :----------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------- |
| Monitor and respond to emails              | Move files to Shared Drives             |
| Generate documents from templates          | Cross-reference data between two Sheets |
| Summarize and classify incoming content    | Run complex multi-step file operations  |
| Route information to Chat/Drive            | Execute custom data transformations     |
| Connect to third-party apps via connectors | Bulk process files with custom logic    |

**Key message:** "Studio handles 80% of your automation needs. For the other 20%, Apps Script bridges the gap — and in today's last module, Gemini writes all of it for you."

### Sharing, Monitoring & Managing (5 min)

**Sharing flows** (different from Drive sharing!):

- Click **Share** → set to "Anyone in your organization with the link can make a copy"
- Recipients get their **own copy** — not access to yours
- Great for distributing best-practice flows across a team

**Monitoring:** My flows → Activity (or Gmail side panel → Flows)
**Managing:** Edit/reorder steps, Turn off instantly, Make a copy to experiment safely

## 📘 Module 4: Apps Script — Gemini Writes the Code, You Click Run ✨ (15 min)

> **Context:** This is your bonus module. You've already built AI colleagues (Gems) and automated flows (Studio). Apps Script is the **magic trick at the end** — the thing that makes your more technical colleagues say "wait, _how_ did you do that?"
>
> **The most important thing to say before starting:**
> _"We are not learning to code today. You don't need to understand a single line of what Gemini produces. You describe the problem, Gemini writes the solution, and you click Run. That's it."_

### 4.1 Why Apps Script? The "Last Mile" Problem (5 min)

There are things Studio just can't do yet:

- ❌ Move files between Shared Drives
- ❌ Pull data from one Sheet into another automatically
- ❌ Run a process exactly on your schedule with custom logic

**Apps Script fills this gap.** And Gemini writes it for you.

**Analogy:** You don't need to know how a car engine works to drive. Gemini is your mechanic. You just tell it where you want to go.

### 4.2 The 3-Step Pattern (5 min)

Every Apps Script automation follows the same 3 steps:

| Step            | What you do                                                      | What Gemini does     |
| :-------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------- |
| 1. **Describe** | Tell Gemini your problem in plain English                        | Writes the code      |
| 2. **Deploy**   | Copy → spreadsheet/doc → Extensions → Apps Script → Paste → Save | Nothing — you did it |
| 3. **Run**      | Click ▶️ Run (authorize once)                                    | The script executes  |

### 4.3 Live Demo: Our Treasury Example (5 min)

> Recall the treasury team's real automation from S9 (February 25): automating a file movement between drives using Apps Script — written entirely by Gemini.

**Prompt used:**

> "Write a Google Apps Script that moves a file from My Drive to a Shared Drive folder. The file ID and folder ID should be variables I can easily change at the top. Add comments explaining each line."

Result: A working script in 90 seconds. Copy → Extensions → Apps Script → Paste → Run.

**Key message:** "The Code is not yours — it's Gemini's. Your job is to clearly describe what you need. The clearer your description, the better the script."

## 🔨 Practice 5: Try It — Automate One Task with Gemini (30 min)

### Step 1: Identify Your Gap (5 min)

Think of something Studio couldn't do for you today, OR one of these common automation needs:

- Moving or copying files between drives
- Pulling data from one Sheet/Doc into another
- Generating a document from a template on a schedule

### Step 2: Describe It to Gemini (10 min)

Use this template:

> "Write a Google Apps Script that [WHAT YOU WANT TO HAPPEN]. The [INPUT/IDs] should be variables at the top I can easily change. Add comments explaining each step."

**Tips:**

- Be specific: "moves a PDF" is better than "handles a file"
- Name your variables: "the source folder ID" not just "the folder"

### Step 3: Deploy & Run (15 min)

1. Copy Gemini's output → Open any Sheet → Extensions → Apps Script → Paste → Run
2. **If it fails:** Copy the error message → paste back to Gemini → "Fix this error: [ERROR]"
3. **If it works:** Show your neighbor. Then optionally add a time trigger (Edit → Triggers → Every day at X AM)

> **Facilitator framing:** "The error → Gemini → fix loop is the most important skill here. Getting an error is _normal_ — it's just iteration. The script you get after fixing 2 errors is always better than the first one."
