> **This is the single most important module in the programme.**

---

## Learning Objective

Every participant leaves with a universal prompting formula they can apply the moment they return to their desk.

---

## The Formula: [Persona] + [Task] + [Context] + [Format]

| Element          | Concept                                                                        | Example                                                                                |
| :--------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. [Persona]** | Give the AI a role. This changes the vocabulary and perspective of the output. | "Act as a strict Vopak SHEQ Auditor." / "Act as a Forensic Project Manager."           |
| **2. [Task]**    | The specific action you need performed. Use strong verbs.                      | "Cross-reference these two documents to find conflicts." / "Perform a 5-Why analysis." |
| **3. [Context]** | The background information or specific documents the AI needs.                 | "Analyze the attached SOP. We are dealing with a highly aggressive supplier."          |
| **4. [Format]**  | How you want the information presented.                                        | "Present in a 3-column table." / "Write as a numbered list with bold headings."        |

---

## Facilitator Notes

- Most beginners treat AI like a Google Search ("Find me X"). We need to train them to treat it like a smart intern — give it a role, a clear job, context, and a desired output format.
- The most common mistake: participants mix up Task and Context. "Task is the action. Context is the background."

---

## Branded Terminology Reminder

> When using the TCF framework for any external communication — customer emails, press releases, regulatory submissions — ensure the Persona and Format elements align with the [Vopak Glossary](../../training/vopak_glossary.md). Vopak's voice: **Trustworthy, Collaborative, Courageous.** AI-drafted text must always be reviewed for tone before it reaches an external audience.

---

## Live Practice: TCF Exercise (15 min)

Each participant writes **one TCF prompt** for a real task from their current role.

Steps:

1. Think of one repetitive or frustrating daily task
2. Fill in the four boxes: Who is the AI? / What should it do? / What does it need to know? / How should it respond?
3. Test the prompt in Gemini
4. 3–4 volunteers share their prompt with the room — facilitator gives live feedback

**Facilitator tip:** If someone is stuck, ask _"What's the most annoying part of your Monday morning?"_ — the answer becomes their Task and Context.
