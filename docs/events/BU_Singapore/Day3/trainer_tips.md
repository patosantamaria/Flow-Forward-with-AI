## ⭐ VIP Strategy: Diego Redonda (President)

### What to Do

1. **M2 (Flow Forward Vision):** After your "Day in the Life" opening, turn to Diego:

   > "Diego, before we continue — as the President of BU Singapore, how do you see AI fitting into the BU's strategic direction?"
   > Let him speak for 2-3 minutes. This builds leadership alignment. The room watches.

2. **During exercises:** Let him participate as a peer. Don't single him out or hover. He should feel like this training is empowering his team, not auditing him.

3. **Closing:** Ask for his takeaway:
   > "Diego, to close us out — what's your one takeaway from today?"
   > His words will carry more weight than anything you've said all day.

### What NOT to Do

- Don't quiz him during basics. He shouldn't feel tested.
- Don't name-drop him excessively. Treat him as a respected colleague, not royalty.
- Don't use hypothetical scenarios that might reveal strategic information ("Imagine Vopak acquired a competitor...").
- Don't present unverified numbers (the 292% ROI was a capstone exercise, not real — he would know).

## Phase 1 Tailoring

### M1: GenAI Foundations — Brief and Credible

**Trim to 12 minutes.** Finance people are efficient. Get to the point.

**Frame it as a business tool:**

> "Gemini is the analytical tool that Vopak has invested in for every employee. It sits inside your Workspace — your Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides. It reads your spreadsheets, summarises your emails, and drafts your documents. It's not a toy — it's infrastructure."

**With the President in the room, emphasise the enterprise investment:**

> "This is part of the EUR 4 billion Shifting Gears programme. Google Workspace with Gemini is the platform Vopak has chosen for every knowledge worker."

### M2: Flow Forward Vision — Finance Pain Points

**Don't say:**

> "AI saves time" (too generic for Finance)

**Say:**

> "Your month-end close involves reconciling data across 6 systems, chasing approvals from 4 departments, formatting reports that nobody reads, and preparing variance explanations for numbers you already understand. What if the first draft of all of that was done before you sat down Monday morning?"

**For Procurement:**

> "You receive 3 vendor quotations. You need a side-by-side comparison: price, delivery terms, payment terms, warranty, SLA compliance. You're building this in Excel from scratch every time. AI does it in 30 seconds."

### M3: Security — Financial Data Sensitivity

**Finance carries the highest data sensitivity after HR:**

> "Never paste actual financial figures — revenue, profit margins, specific customer deal values — into any AI tool. Instead, use relative data: 'Revenue increased by 15%' instead of 'Revenue was EUR 127 million.' Or anonymise: 'Department A's budget' instead of 'Finance SG's budget.'"

**Procurement addition:**

> "Vendor pricing is confidential. When comparing quotations with AI, remove vendor names and use 'Vendor A, B, C.' Never paste actual contract values."

**SOD (Segregation of Duties):**

> "If you use AI to draft approval recommendations or financial analyses, the AI output is a draft. The approval, decision, and sign-off are always human. SOD principles apply exactly the same way — AI doesn't change your control framework."

### M4: PTCF — Financial Framing

**Use a Finance-specific "Bad Briefing":**

> Say: "Analyse this data."
> They'll ask: "What data? What are we analysing for? What period? What format do you want the output in?"
> "That's PTCF."

**This lands faster with Finance** because they write analysis briefs constantly. They'll get it in one round.

## Energy Management

**Morning:** High energy. Finance groups are engaged if you're precise. Lose them if you're vague.

**Diego's presence:** Lifts the room's seriousness. People will be slightly more formal. This is fine — lean into professionalism rather than humour.

**Post-lunch:** With 14 people post-lunch, energy dips. The Mobile demo (M8) re-engages physically. The voice-to-meeting-summary is useful for Finance managers who attend back-to-back meetings.

**Capstone:** 14 people = 3 teams of 4-5. The "Investment Radar" capstone appeals to their analytical instincts. Frame it as "your CAPEX analysis for the Investment Committee."
