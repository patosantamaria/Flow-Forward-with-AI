### 🧠 09:55 — M4: PTCF Prompting Framework (25 min)

**Tailored PTCF Demo for Safety/Operations:**

| Element     | Concept    | Safety Example                                                       | Ops Example                                                        | Cust Svc Example                                             |
| :---------- | :--------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Persona** | Role       | "Act as a Vopak SHEQ Investigator"                                   | "Act as a Vopak Operations Superintendent"                         | "Act as a Vopak Customer Service Manager"                    |
| **Task**    | Action     | "Structure these witness statements into a formal incident report"   | "Summarize these shift notes into a handover report"               | "Draft a response to this customer complaint"                |
| **Context** | Background | "4 witness statements from a near-miss at Bay 3, gasket leak on MLA" | "Raw notes from night shift, 3 active PTWs, 1 defeated instrument" | "Customer reports delayed unloading, SLE compliance at risk" |
| **Format**  | Output     | "Vopak Macro Incident Format: timeline, 5-Why, HiPo classification"  | "Sections: Safety, Equipment, PTWs, Jetty, Pending Maintenance"    | "Professional,empathetic tone, reference SLE standards"      |

**The "Bad Briefing" Game:**

Say:

> "Write a safety report."

Wait. Terminal Managers and Safety officers will immediately ask: "What incident? What happened? What site? What classification?"

> "You just did PTCF. You asked for the context every good investigator needs."

**Live PTCF comparison:**

**Bad prompt:**

> "Write a safety report about a leak."

**PTCF prompt:**

> "**[Persona]** Act as a Vopak SHEQ Investigator conducting a near-miss investigation.
> **[Task]** Structure these witness statements into a formal incident report.
> **[Context]** Near-miss at Sakra terminal Bay 3. Gasket failure on Marine Loading Arm A during HMD product transfer. No release detected by LEL monitors but visual mist observed. Operations isolated the line and initiated emergency PtW. 3 witness statements attached.
> **[Format]** Use the Vopak Macro Incident Format: (1) Incident Timeline (minute-by-minute), (2) Root Cause Analysis using 5-Why method, (3) Immediate Corrective Actions with owners, (4) Preventive Actions with deadlines, (5) HiPo classification with justification."

### 🏆 14:50 — Capstone: "The Procedure Flow" (55 min)

> **Source:** [`sheq/capstone.md`](../../departments/sheq/capstone.md)

**Brief the challenge (5 min):**

> "Here's your scenario: Vopak Singapore is about to receive HMD — a new product — at the Sakra terminal. The existing truck loading SOP is generic and doesn't cover HMD-specific requirements: elevated temperatures, specialised PPE, specific gasket materials, and vapour management during transfer. You need to create a product-specific SOP that is safe, practical, and ready for field use."
>
> "You have 50 minutes. You need to deliver two things: a field-ready SOP for HMD truck loading, and a Toolbox Talk script that a Shift Supervisor can deliver in 10 minutes."

**Data Room:**

- Existing SOP (Legacy — generic, with gaps)
- HMD Safety Data Sheet
- Incident Investigation Report (anonymised near-miss with similar product)
- Regulatory Requirements Summary
- PPE Classification Matrix

**Team Split:**
2 teams of 5. Team A: Safety-led (Gary, Derek lead). Team B: Terminal Manager-led (Lun Pin, Ravin lead). Lynn, Pearlene, Jesslyn, Jinxin, Awyong distributed across teams.

**Step 1: NotebookLM (10 min)** — Upload Data Room. Ask: "What are the critical gaps in the existing SOP for handling HMD product?"

**Step 2: Deep Research (10 min):**

> "Based on the HMD Safety Data Sheet, identify all additional safety requirements compared to a standard petroleum product: PPE changes, temperature control requirements, emergency response differences, and environmental containment needs."

**Step 3: Drafting (20 min):**

> **Deliverable 1 — Product-Specific SOP:** "Draft a Truck Loading Standard Operating Procedure for HMD product at Vopak Singapore Sakra terminal. Structure: Pre-Loading Checks (PPE, equipment, line verification), Loading Procedure (step-by-step with safety gates), Post-Loading Procedures (line clearing, documentation), Emergency Response (specific to HMD). Each step must include: Action, Responsible Role, Safety Check, and Stop Point criteria."

> **Deliverable 2 — Toolbox Talk Script:** "Draft a 10-minute Toolbox Talk script for the shift team covering: what is HMD (properties, hazards), what's different about this product vs. what we normally handle, key PPE requirements, the critical steps they must follow, and what to do if something goes wrong. Tone: practical, straightforward, field-ready — no corporate jargon."

**Step 4: Quick Review (10 min)** — Teams swap SOPs and each identifies 2 gaps in the other team's procedure.

**Mid-Challenge Update (at 25 min mark):**

> "Update from the Safety team: a review of the SDS reveals that HMD has a lower flash point than initially assumed. Any loading procedure must now include continuous LEL monitoring with an alarm threshold at 10% LEL (lower than our standard 20%). Adjust your SOP."
