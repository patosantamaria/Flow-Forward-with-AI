## ⭐ VIP Strategy: Brandon Friend (SVP Ops & Technical)

### What to Do

1. **M2 (Flow Forward Vision):** After the "Day in the Life" opening:

   > "Brandon, as SVP Ops — how do you see AI fitting into terminal operations? What's your biggest time sink that could benefit from this?"

- Don't challenge safety practices. Operations people are protective of their safety culture — rightly so. AI supports safety, never replaces safety judgement.
- Don't suggest AI can replace Terminal Managers. Frame it as "your administrative assistant, not your replacement."

## Phase 1 Tailoring

### 🌍 The Bigger Picture — MUST deliver in M1

**This is a BU Singapore requirement.** Before moving into M2, set the broader AI context:

> "What we cover today is the foundation — AI is much bigger. Agentic AI, autonomous monitoring, real-time anomaly detection — that's where the industry is heading. Today gets everyone ready."

**For Safety/Ops, anchor it in their world:**

> "Imagine AI agents that monitor terminal sensors 24/7, detect pressure anomalies, generate the alert, and draft the incident report — in real time. That's coming. And YOUR safety expertise is what makes you the best people to evaluate how it should be used in the field."

**Important for Brandon:** This message reinforces that AI is a strategic investment, not a fad. His buy-in here legitimises the broader programme.

### M1: GenAI Foundations — Field-First Framing

**This group doesn't sit at desks all day.** Terminal Managers and Safety Officers are in the field 50%+ of the time.

**Replace this:**

> "Gemini works inside your Gmail and Docs"

**With this:**

> "You just finished a 12-hour shift. You have 30 minutes worth of handover notes to write. Your superintendent is waiting. What if you could speak your notes into your phone and get a structured, formatted handover report back in 60 seconds? That's what Gemini does."

**Start with Mobile, not Desktop.** For this group, the phone is the primary device.

### M2: Flow Forward Vision — Operational Pain Points

**Frame through their daily frustrations:**

> "How much time do you spend on shift handovers, incident reports, SOP reviews, and formatting inspection notes? Some of you told me it's 30-40% of your day. The rest of your day is actual operations. What if AI handled the paperwork so you could focus on the terminal?"

**For Safety Officers:**

> "Every near-miss report takes 30-60 minutes to structure properly. AI can turn witness statements and raw notes into a formatted investigation report in 3 minutes. You spend your time analysing the root cause, not formatting tables."

### M3: Security — Field-Specific Dangers

**This is critical for operational staff:**

> "Never photograph or share: SCADA screens, DCS configurations, P&IDs with confidential process data, security camera footage, or anything showing access codes, gate procedures, or manifests with customer product details."

**Positive framing:**

> "What you CAN share: generic safety observations (remove identifying info), SOP text for review, training materials, and non-confidential process descriptions. When in doubt, strip the identifying details."

### M4: PTCF — Operational Game

**Use an operations-specific "Bad Briefing":**

> Say: "Write a report."
> Terminal Managers will immediately ask: "What kind of report? For what incident? What format does the superintendent want? What's the deadline?"
> "That's PTCF. You just wrote the perfect AI prompt."

**For Safety Officers:**

> Say: "Analyse this safety data."
> They'll ask: "What data? What time period? What are we looking for — trends, root causes, classified events?"
> "Exactly. That's the Context in PTCF."

## Energy Management

**Morning:** Mixed energy. Friday + operations audience = start with something practical and engaging. The mobile voice-to-report demo is a great opener energy boost.

**The Visual Analysis Moment:** When you upload a terminal photo and Gemini identifies safety observations — this is the jaw-dropping moment. **Don't rush it.** Let the room react. Ask the Safety Officers: "Did it catch everything you'd catch?" This generates discussion and validates the tool.

**Post-lunch:** With 10 people after lunch on a Friday, energy will dip. The capstone needs to be immediately engaging. Frame it as: "Last challenge of the week. Show us what you've learned."

**Closing (Friday 3:45 PM):** This is the end of the entire BU Singapore training week. Make the closing matter:

> "You've spent this week learning tools that will change how you work. You now have an advantage that most of the industry doesn't have yet. Use it. Start Monday."

## The Mobile Demo — Your Star Moment

**For this group, Mobile is everything.** Terminal Managers don't sit at desks.

**Extended Mobile Demo (20 min instead of 15):**

1. Voice-to-text: "Gemini, summarise my shift: Tank 405 completed transfer at 14:30, no incidents. Maintenance team replaced gasket on V-201. Night shift needs to monitor pressure on T-802." → Formatted handover report
2. Photo analysis: Take a photo of a printed document or whiteboard → extracted, structured text
3. Quick-fire Gmail: Summarise and respond to emails between inspections

**Let Brandon or a Terminal Manager try it live.** Hand them your phone (or theirs). Let them speak a shift note. Show the formatted output. This is the most powerful demo of the week for this group.

## Common Questions from Operations Groups

| Question                                             | Recommended Response                                                                                                                                                                  |
| :--------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Can AI replace safety inspections?"                 | "Never. AI assists — humans decide. AI can help you write reports faster and spot patterns in data, but the safety walk, the human judgement, the experience — that's irreplaceable." |
| "What about shift workers who don't have computers?" | "Gemini works on any smartphone. Voice input, photo input, quick summaries — all from your pocket. No laptop needed."                                                                 |
| "Can we use photos from the terminal?"               | "Yes, for general safety observations. Remove or avoid capturing: SCADA screens, access codes, security cameras, customer-identifiable product labels."                               |
| "What about in control rooms?"                       | "Gemini in Sheets and Docs can help with data analysis and report writing from the control room. But never put DCS/SCADA data into AI tools."                                         |
| "We barely use email — is this relevant?"            | "Absolutely. The biggest wins for operations aren't email — they're shift handovers, incident reports, SOP reviews, and safety observations. All voice-to-text on your phone."        |
