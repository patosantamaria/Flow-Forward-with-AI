# Day 5 — NotebookLM: Slide Deck Outline

## Audience: Safety + Terminal Management + Customer Service + Engineering + HR (10 pax)

### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "NotebookLM — Your Team's Brain"
- Subtitle: "Upload your documents. Ask questions. Get source-cited answers."

### Slide 2: What Makes NotebookLM Different

- Other AI tools guess. NotebookLM quotes.
- Every answer includes source citations — exact paragraphs and page numbers
- Your documents stay private — only you access your notebook
- For Operations: upload SOPs, safety standards, ISGOTT checklists — any shift supervisor can query at 2 AM

### Slide 3: How It Works

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload sources (Google Docs, PDFs, Drive files, websites)
- Step 3: Ask questions — get grounded, cited answers
- Operational superpower: "What's the safe work procedure for hot work in Zone 1?" → exact procedure with source

### Slide 4: Demo — Grounded Safety Q&A

- Upload: Safety procedures + ISGOTT checklist + incident reports
- Prompt shown: "According to these documents, what is the exact procedure for responding to a gasket failure during product transfer of a Class 3 flammable liquid? Include initial isolation steps, notification requirements, and incident report format."
- Result: Step-by-step procedure with exact source citations
- Safety impact: "Your safety expert, on every shift, with the right answer every time."

### Slide 5: Demo — SOP Version Comparison

- Upload: Current SOP + revised SOP
- Prompt shown: "Compare these two versions. List every change: New Step, Modified Step, or Removed Step. Flag any changes that affect safety classifications or PtW requirements."
- Show: Colour-coded comparison with dual citations
- Audio Overview: "Imagine the night shift listening to a summary of the new SOP changes during their commute"

### Slide 6: Your Turn — Practice

- Step 1: Create a team notebook
- Step 2: Upload safety or operational documents from your Drive
- Step 3: Ask: "What procedure covers [something relevant to your shift]?"
- Step 4: Try Audio Overview
- Share: "What document would your night shift supervisor want in this notebook?"

### Slide 7: Key Takeaway

- "For Safety & Operations: your institutional memory — always available, always cited."
- "Build a team notebook with SOPs, safety standards, and incident reports."
- "Any shift, any question, any time — with the exact source."
- "The night shift supervisor's best friend."
