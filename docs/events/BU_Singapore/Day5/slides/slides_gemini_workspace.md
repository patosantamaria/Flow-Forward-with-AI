### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "Gemini in Workspace — Your Side Panel Assistant"
- Subtitle: "AI built into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Slides — where you already work"

### Slide 2: What Is the Side Panel?

- The Gemini icon in Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Slides
- One click opens your AI assistant — reads the document you're looking at
- Key rule: "If it's in your Drive, Gemini can read it. Use `@` to reference files."
- For operations: "Think of it as your operations assistant — right inside the tools you already use"

### Slide 3: Demo — Gmail (Operational Email Triage)

- Scenario: Email thread about an upcoming maintenance shutdown and its impact on terminal operations
- Prompt shown: "Summarize this email thread. What are the key dates? What systems are affected? What actions are required from terminal operations?"
- Result: Structured timeline with action items
- Ops use case: maintenance coordination, shift-critical communications, safety alerts

### Slide 4: Demo — Docs (Shift Handover Structuring)

- Scenario: Raw shift notes from the night shift — unstructured, incomplete
- Prompt shown: "Act as a Vopak Operations Superintendent. Structure these raw shift notes into a formal Daily Shift Handover Report. Sections: Safety Incidents & Near-Misses, Equipment Status (especially defeated safety-critical equipment), Active Permits to Work, Ongoing Jetty Activities, Pending Maintenance Tasks with priority."
- Result: Structured handover report ready for the incoming superintendent
- Ops impact: "30 minutes of formatting → 3 minutes"

### Slide 5: Demo — Customer Service Email Response

- Scenario: Customer complaint about a delayed truck loading
- Prompt shown: "Draft a professional, empathetic response to a customer complaint about a 4-hour truck loading delay. Acknowledge the issue, explain the cause, offer a next step, reference our SLE commitment."
- Customer Service impact: "Respond faster, with the right tone, every time"

### Slide 6: Your Turn — Practice Exercises

- Safety team: Near-miss incident structuring (Docs) — Structure witness statements into a formal report — 10 min
- Terminal Managers: Shift handover drafting (Docs) — Raw notes → structured report — 10 min
- Customer Service: Customer response drafting (Gmail) — Professional, empathetic reply — 10 min
- Others: Choose an exercise relevant to your role or freestyle

### Slide 7: Key Takeaway

- "For Safety: structure incident reports in minutes, not hours."
- "For Terminal Managers: shift handovers that used to take 30 minutes — done in 3."
- "For Customer Service: faster replies with the right professional tone."
