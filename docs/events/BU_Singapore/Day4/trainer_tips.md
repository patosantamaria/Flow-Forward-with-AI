# 🎯 Day 4 — Trainer Tips & Tailoring Guide

## Thursday: Commercial + Business Development (12 pax)

---

## Audience Read

**The most competitive group of the week.** Commercial people are wired for speed, persuasion, and winning. They'll be engaged if you frame everything as a competitive advantage. They'll disengage the moment something feels theoretical. Every example must tie back to deals, customers, and revenue.

**Key personalities to watch for:**

- **Neeraj, Sherif, Vicknesh, Chen Howe, Mikal** — Sales-oriented. They want to close deals faster. Show them how AI gives them hours back.
- **Angeline, Jeanna, Charlene** — May lean more operational/support. Ensure exercises work for their roles too (customer comms, reporting, data analysis).
- **This is a homogeneous group** — All Commercial/BD. This is actually easier to tailor because every example resonates with everyone. Lean into it.

---

## Phase 1 Tailoring

### M1: GenAI Foundations — Speed Frame

**Don't give a tech lecture.** This group doesn't care how the engine works — they care how fast the car goes.

**Replace this:**

> "Gemini is a Large Language Model that processes text..."

**With this:**

> "Here's what matters: Gemini reads emails in seconds, analyses 45-page RFPs in minutes, and drafts customer proposals while you're still opening the document. Today I'm going to show you how to use it as your competitive advantage."

**30 seconds, max.** Then move to the demo.

### M2: Flow Forward Vision — Frame as Deal Velocity

**Don't say:**

> "AI improves productivity"

**Say:**

> "Your competitor across the road on Jurong Island received the same RFP you did. They'll spend 2 days analysing it. You'll do it in 20 minutes. You respond faster, with better analysis, and win the deal. That's what today is about."

**This group responds to competitive framing.** Use it throughout the day:

> "The deal goes to the team that responds first with the best analysis. AI is your speed advantage."

### M3: Security — Customer Data Sensitivity

**Commercial handles highly sensitive customer data:**

> "Never paste customer names, deal values, contract specifics, or pricing into prompts. Use 'Customer A' instead of the actual name. Use 'approximately $X million' instead of exact figures. Your customer expects confidentiality — AI doesn't change that obligation."

**Quick, sharp, done.** This group won't sit through a long security lecture. Make the point and move on.

### M4: PTCF — The "Bad RFP Response" Game

**This is your best PTCF demo for Commercial:**

> Say: "Write a proposal."
> They'll immediately ask: "For which customer? What product? What capacity? What timeline? What's our competitive position?"
> "You just wrote a PTCF prompt. Persona: Vopak Senior Commercial Manager. Task: Draft a storage proposal. Context: [customer, product, capacity, competition]. Format: Executive summary with pricing table."

**This lands in one round.** Don't belabour it. Commercial people learn by doing, not by discussing.

## Energy Management

**Morning:** High energy from the start. Commercial people are talkers. Channel the energy into demos rather than discussion (or you'll lose control).

**Lunchtime:** This group will network over lunch. Don't cut it short — the conversations they have over lunch about AI will reinforce the morning session.

**Capstone:** Frame as a **competition**. "Three teams. Best RFP analysis wins." This group will sprint. The competitive energy is your friend.

---

## The Competitive Capstone Edge

For the "Speed to Deal" capstone, lean into competition:

> "Three teams. Same RFP. Same 45 minutes. The team with the best customer presentation wins. I'm judging on: Analysis Depth, Speed, Presentation Quality, and Competitive Insight."

**Keep score visibly.** Write team names on a whiteboard. Update scores after each phase. This group LIVES for competition.

**Warning:** Don't let competition override learning. If a team is struggling, pause and coach them privately. Say: "This isn't about winning — it's about building a skill you'll use every day." Then let them compete again.

## Common Questions from Commercial Groups

| Question                                         | Recommended Response                                                                                                                                  |
| :----------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Can I use this to analyse a real customer RFP?" | "Yes, today. Remove customer names and use 'Customer A.' But the analysis is immediately applicable."                                                 |
| "Can it write proposals for me?"                 | "It drafts proposals in your style. You review, refine, and own the output. Think of it as your analyst — you're still the salesperson."              |
| "What about customer-facing content?"            | "AI-drafted content should always be reviewed and personalised before sending to customers. The first draft is AI. The final version is you."         |
| "Can I use this on my phone while travelling?"   | "Yes — Gemini app on mobile. Draft emails, summarise documents, prep for meetings — all from the taxi to the customer."                               |
| "How do I beat the competition with this?"       | "Speed and depth. You respond to RFPs faster with better analysis. You walk into customer meetings with research they don't expect. That's the edge." |
