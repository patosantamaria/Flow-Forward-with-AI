### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "NotebookLM — Your Team's Brain"
- Subtitle: "Upload your documents. Ask questions. Get source-cited answers."

### Slide 2: What Makes NotebookLM Different

- Other AI tools guess. NotebookLM quotes.
- Every answer includes source citations — exact clauses and page numbers
- Your documents stay private — only you access your notebook
- For Commercial: upload pricing matrices, T&Cs, SLE definitions — query them before any negotiation

### Slide 3: How It Works

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload sources (Google Docs, PDFs, Drive files, websites)
- Step 3: Ask questions — get grounded, cited answers
- Commercial superpower: "What's our standard position on exclusivity clauses?" → exact clause + paragraph

### Slide 4: Demo — T&C Deep Dive

- Upload: Vopak standard T&Cs + customer's proposed terms
- Prompt shown: "Cross-reference these two documents. Identify every clause where the customer's terms deviate from our standard. For each: cite clause numbers, classify risk, suggest our position."
- Result: Deviation matrix with dual citations from both documents
- Commercial impact: "Contract comparison that normally takes 2 hours — done in 30 seconds"

### Slide 5: Demo — Deal Intelligence Base

- Upload: Pricing matrix + terminal capability document
- Prompt shown: "What storage capacity do we have available for a customer needing 20,000 cbm of HMD? What are the pricing tiers and facility specs?"
- Show: building a "deal prep" notebook for any customer meeting

### Slide 6: Your Turn — Practice

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload commercial documents from your Drive (pricing, T&Cs, capability lists)
- Step 3: Ask 3 deal-relevant questions
- Step 4: Try Audio Overview — "Your competitor briefing as a podcast"
- Share: "What document would save you the most prep time before a customer meeting?"

### Slide 7: Key Takeaway

- "For Commercial: your deal intelligence base. Every answer sourced, every clause cited."
- "Build a 'Master Deal Notebook' with pricing, T&Cs, and capabilities. Query it before every negotiation."
