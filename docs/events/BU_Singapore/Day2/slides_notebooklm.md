### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "NotebookLM — Your Team's Brain"
- Subtitle: "Upload your documents. Ask questions. Get source-cited answers."

### Slide 2: What Makes NotebookLM Different

- Other AI tools guess. NotebookLM quotes.
- Every answer includes source citations — exact paragraphs and page numbers
- Your documents stay private — only you access your notebook
- For Engineering: upload standards, SOPs, vendor manuals, and specs — query them instantly

### Slide 3: How It Works

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload sources (Google Docs, PDFs, Drive files, websites, YouTube)
- Step 3: Ask questions — get grounded, cited answers
- Up to 50 sources per notebook, up to 500,000 words each

### Slide 4: Demo — Technical Standards Q&A

- Upload: Engineering standards document + project specifications
- Prompt shown: "What are the requirements for fire suppression systems in Zone 1 hazardous areas according to these documents? Include specific clause references."
- Result: Cited answer with exact clause numbers
- Engineering impact: "Instead of searching a 300-page standard, get the answer in 10 seconds with the source."

### Slide 5: Demo — Cross-Document Analysis

- Upload: Vendor SoW + Vopak project standard + risk assessment template
- Prompt shown: "Compare this vendor SoW against our project standard. Where does it fall short? Cite specific sections."
- Show the source comparison with colour-coded citations

### Slide 6: Demo — Audio Overview

- Turn dense technical standards into a digestible podcast-style summary
- Use case: "New engineer joining a project can listen to a summary of the 5 key documents before Day 1"
- Live demo: Generate Audio Overview from uploaded engineering documents

### Slide 7: Your Turn — Practice

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload 2-3 technical documents from your Drive (standards, SOPs, vendor docs)
- Step 3: Ask 3 technical questions
- Step 4: Try the Audio Overview
- Share: "What document would save your team the most time if they could just ask it questions?"

### Slide 8: Key Takeaway

- "For Engineering: your standards library, queryable in seconds."
