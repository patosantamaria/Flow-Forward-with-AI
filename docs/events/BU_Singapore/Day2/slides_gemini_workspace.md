### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "Gemini in Workspace — Your Side Panel Assistant"
- Subtitle: "AI built into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Slides — where you already work"

### Slide 2: What Is the Side Panel?

- The Gemini icon in Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Slides
- One click opens your AI assistant — reads the document you're looking at
- Key rule: "If it's in your Drive, Gemini can read it. Use `@` to reference files."
- For this technical group: think of it as an intelligent context window over your working documents

### Slide 3: Demo — Gmail (Technical Email Triage)

- Scenario: Multi-thread email chain about a vendor SoW with Engineering specs, IT security questions, and timeline issues
- Prompt shown: "Summarize this email thread. What technical decisions were made? What are the outstanding questions? List every action item with the owner's name."
- Result: Clean summary with decisions and action items extracted
- Engineering use case: vendor correspondence, project update chains

### Slide 4: Demo — Docs (SoW Review)

- Scenario: Statement of Work for a control system upgrade
- Prompt shown: "Act as a Senior Vopak Project Engineer. Review this SoW and identify: vague deliverables, missing acceptance criteria, scope gaps, and any clauses that don't align with Vopak's standard project governance."
- The `@` connector: pulling in the Vopak standard SoW template for comparison
- Engineering impact: "First-pass SoW review in 3 minutes instead of 2 hours"

### Slide 5: Demo — Sheets (Maintenance Data Analysis)

- Scenario: Equipment maintenance log with dates, costs, and failure types
- Prompt shown: "Analyse this maintenance data. Identify: equipment with the highest failure frequency, total cost trends over the last 12 months, and any patterns that suggest preventive maintenance scheduling changes."
- IT/Engineering use case: asset management data, project tracking, budget analysis

### Slide 6: Your Turn — Practice Exercises

- Exercise 1: SoW Stress Test (Docs) — Review a technical SoW for scope gaps and vague deliverables — 10 min
- Exercise 2: Risk Assessment Structuring (Docs) — Structure a risk register from raw meeting notes — 10 min
- Exercise 3: Maintenance Data Analysis (Sheets) — Identify patterns in equipment failure data — 10 min
- "Use the exact prompts on the handout. Adapt them to your actual projects."

### Slide 7: Key Takeaway

- "For Engineering and IT: the Side Panel is your first-pass analyst."
- "Gmail: Extract decisions from technical threads. Docs: Review and stress-test documents. Sheets: Spot patterns in operational data."
