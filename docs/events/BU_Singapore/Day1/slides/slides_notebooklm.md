# Day 1 — NotebookLM: Slide Deck Outline

## Audience: HR + Office Management + DSAM VAS (9 pax)

---

### Slide 1: Module Title

- Title: "NotebookLM — Your Team's Brain"
- Subtitle: "Upload your documents. Ask questions. Get source-cited answers."

### Slide 2: What Makes NotebookLM Different

- Other AI tools guess. NotebookLM quotes.
- Every answer comes with source citations — exact paragraphs, page numbers
- Your documents stay private — only you can access your notebook
- Perfect for: policy lookups, compliance checks, handbook Q&A, cross-document analysis

### Slide 3: How It Works

- Step 1: Create a notebook
- Step 2: Upload sources (Google Docs, PDFs, Drive files, websites, YouTube)
- Step 3: Ask questions — get grounded, cited answers
- Key visual: Upload → Ask → Get cited answer with source highlight

### Slide 4: Demo — HR Policy Expert (Grounded Q&A)

- Upload: Employee Handbook + L&D Policy
- Prompt shown: "What is the exact procedure for approving a cross-border relocation? What are the eligibility criteria and required documentation?"
- Result: Answer with exact paragraph citations from the handbook
- HR impact: "Your team's 24/7 policy expert. Any HR question, any time, with the right source."

### Slide 5: Demo — Audio Overview

- Turn your 120-page Employee Handbook into a 10-minute podcast
- New hires can listen during their commute instead of reading a dense PDF
- Live demo: Generate Audio Overview from uploaded documents
- "Imagine the onboarding experience if every new joiner could listen to the key policies before Day 1"

### Slide 6: Your Turn — Practice

- Step 1: Create a new notebook
- Step 2: Upload 2-3 HR documents you already have in your Drive
- Step 3: Ask 3 increasingly specific questions
- Step 4: Try generating an Audio Overview
- Share: "What document would you put in your team's permanent notebook?"

### Slide 7: Key Takeaway

- "NotebookLM is your institutional memory. Upload once, query forever."
- "For HR: Employee Handbook, L&D policies, Works Council guidelines, onboarding templates — all in one searchable, citable brain."
- "Next step: Share the notebook with your team. They stop asking you policy questions."
