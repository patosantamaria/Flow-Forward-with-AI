# 🎯 Day 1 — Trainer Tips & Tailoring Guide

## Monday: HR + Office Management + DSAM VAS (9 pax)

---

## Audience Read

This is your **warmest, most receptive group**. HR professionals are natural people-readers — they'll pick up on your energy immediately. They're also the most likely to feel anxious about AI ("will it replace us?"), so your first job is to disarm that fear.

**Key personalities to watch for:**

- **The Sceptic** — Likely 1-2 people who've heard AI horror stories about bias in recruitment. Address it head-on in M3 (Security). Don't dodge it.
- **The Enthusiast** — Usually 1-2 HR people who've already tried ChatGPT at home. Channel them — they become your in-room advocates.

---

## Phase 1 Tailoring

### M1: GenAI Foundations — Phrasing Tips

**Replace this:**

> "AI can process data faster than humans"

**With this:**

> "Think of Gemini as the world's most well-read intern. They've read everything — every policy document, every best practice, every framework. But they've never worked at Vopak. They don't know your culture, your people, your specific context. That's what YOU bring."

**Why:** HR thinks in terms of people, not data. The "intern" analogy resonates because they manage interns.

**Extra for this group:**

> "And just like a real intern, if you give it a vague brief, you'll get vague work back. If you brief it like you'd brief a new team member — with context, expectations, and format — you'll get excellent work."

### M2: Flow Forward Vision — Emotional Anchoring

This is where you win or lose the room. HR people are **emotionally driven professionals** — they chose HR because they care about people.

**Don't say:**

> "AI will make you more productive"

**Say:**

> "Right now, you spend half your day doing things a machine could do — copying data between systems, formatting documents, searching PDFs for policy clauses. That's not what you were hired for. You were hired to coach leaders, design culture, develop people. AI gives you back the time to do the work that actually matters."

**Pause after this.** Let it land. You'll see nods.

### M3: Security — The HR-Specific Dangers

**Make this personal and scary (briefly):**

> "Imagine someone asks Gemini: 'Draft a termination letter for Sarah in Finance who has been underperforming for 6 months and has had 2 PIPs.' That prompt just exposed PII, performance data, and a potential legal liability — all in one sentence. The correct version: 'Draft a termination letter template for a role in a corporate function, referencing 2 performance improvement cycles.'"

**Works Council angle:** If anyone in the group has Works Council experience or awareness, lean into it:

> "Any AI-generated content that could affect employee evaluations, disciplinary processes, or workforce planning should always be reviewed against Works Council guidelines. This isn't just good practice — it's a regulatory requirement in many of our geographies."

### M4: PTCF — The "Bad Briefing" Game

**This game REALLY works with HR people.** They brief people all day long. When you say "I need an email" and they immediately ask "To who? About what? What tone?", the entire room will laugh and get it instantly.

**Go longer on this game with this group** — let 3-4 people ask their clarifying questions before you reveal the PTCF link. The more questions they ask, the stronger the "aha."

## Common Questions from HR Groups

| Question                           | Recommended Response                                                                                                                                                                         |
| :--------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Can we use AI to screen CVs?"     | "Yes, for initial sorting and summarising. Never as the sole decision-maker. Always human review before any candidate is progressed or rejected."                                            |
| "What about bias?"                 | "Gemini can reproduce biases from its training data. That's why the Persona matters — 'Act as a Vopak diversity-focused recruiter' changes the output dramatically. Always review for bias." |
| "Can employees see if we used AI?" | "Transparency is key. The ethical guidelines you'll draft in the capstone should address this. Best practice: disclose when AI assisted the process."                                        |
| "Will this replace HR jobs?"       | "No. The CEO mandate is explicitly 'reskilling, not headcount reduction.' AI handles the admin — you do the human work. The people who use AI will replace the people who don't."            |
