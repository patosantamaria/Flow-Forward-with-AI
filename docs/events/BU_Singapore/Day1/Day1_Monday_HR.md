### 🧠 09:55 — M4: PTCF Prompting Framework (25 min)

> Follow the standard M4 framework from [`phase1_foundation_ethics.md`](../../phases/phase1_foundation_ethics.md#-m4--the-ptcf-prompting-framework-35-min-incl-practice).

**Tailored PTCF Demo for HR:**

**Step 1 — Show the formula:**

| Element     | Concept                 | HR Example                                                                            |
| :---------- | :---------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Persona** | Give the AI a role      | "Act as a Vopak HR Business Partner"                                                  |
| **Task**    | The specific action     | "Draft a 30-day onboarding checklist"                                                 |
| **Context** | Background info         | "New Terminal Operations Engineer, Singapore region, starts Monday"                   |
| **Format**  | How you want the output | "Weekly plan with columns: Week, Activity, Owner, Prerequisites, Completion Criteria" |

**Step 2 — The "Bad Briefing" Game (5 min):**

Say to the room:

> "I need an email."

Wait. Let the room react. They'll ask: "To who? About what? When?"

> "Exactly. You just did PTCF. You asked me for Person, Task, Context, Format. That's all prompting is — giving the AI the same context you'd give a new team member."

**Step 3 — Live PTCF Example (show on screen):**

**Bad prompt:**

> "Write an onboarding email."

Show the generic, useless result.

**PTCF prompt:**

> "**[Persona]** Act as a Vopak HR Business Partner responsible for Singapore onboarding.
> **[Task]** Draft a welcoming first-day email for a new Terminal Operations Engineer arriving next Monday.
> **[Context]** They will report to the Operations Superintendent at the Sakra terminal. First week includes SHEQ induction, IS-Safe tablet training, and IT setup. Their buddy is a senior operator named Wei.
> **[Format]** Warm but professional tone. Include: welcome message, first-day logistics (arrival time, building entry, parking), Week 1 agenda overview, buddy introduction, and who to contact for IT issues."

Show the dramatically better result.
