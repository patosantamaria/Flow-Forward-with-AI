# Singapore MOM — Employment Regulations Summary

## Relevant to Terminal Operations Shift Workers

> **Source:** Ministry of Manpower (MOM), Employment Act (Part IV), Workplace Safety & Health Act
> **Compiled for:** Vopak Singapore HR — Capstone Exercise Reference
> **Note:** This is a simplified summary. Always consult the full Act for authoritative guidance.

---

## Employment Act — Part IV: Rest Days, Hours of Work, Overtime

### Applicability

Part IV applies to **all employees** earning a basic monthly salary of **$4,500 or less**, and to **all manual labourers** regardless of salary.

> ⚠️ Terminal Operators at $3,500 base are covered by Part IV.

### Working Hours

- Maximum **8 hours/day** or **44 hours/week** for standard schedules
- For shift workers on 12-hour shifts: allowed under **Section 40** with MOM approval
- **Total working hours (including overtime) must not exceed 12 hours in any one day**

### Overtime

- Overtime rate: **1.5x** the hourly basic rate
- **Monthly overtime cap: 72 hours**
- Overtime beyond 72 hours requires MOM exemption (rarely granted)

### Rest Days

- Minimum **1 rest day per week** (unpaid for daily-rated, included in salary for monthly-rated)
- Rest day work (employer's request): **2x** basic daily rate
- Rest day work (employee's request): **1x** (up to half shift), **2x** (beyond half shift)

### Public Holidays

- Singapore gazetted public holidays: **11 per year**
- If required to work: **200%** of daily rate + 1 day off in lieu (or 300% with no day off)

## Workplace Safety & Health Act (WSH Act)

### Employer Obligations

- Conduct **risk assessments** for all workplaces
- Provide adequate **safety training** before employees handle hazardous substances
- Maintain **Safety Data Sheets (SDS)** for all chemicals on-site
- Report workplace accidents to MOM within **10 days** (or immediately for dangerous occurrences)

### Hazardous Chemical Handling

- Workers handling Class 8 (corrosive) or Class 3 (flammable) chemicals must hold:
  - **Workplace Safety & Health certification** (accredited training provider)
  - **Chemical handling competency** specific to the product class
  - **First aid certification** (BCLS + AED recommended)
- PPE requirements must match the chemical's SDS specifications
- **Level 3 PPE** (full-face respiratory protection, chemical suit) required for:
  - Products with vapour pressure above threshold
  - Products classified as corrosive with inhalation hazard
  - Operations involving open transfer or sampling

### PPE Certification Timeline

- Level 1 PPE training: **1 day** (standard industrial PPE)
- Level 2 PPE training: **3 days** (chemical-specific respiratory protection)
- Level 3 PPE training: **2 weeks** (full chemical suit, SCBA, decontamination procedures)

## Skills Development Levy (SDL)

- Employer pays **0.25% of employee's monthly remuneration**, minimum $2/month, maximum $11.25/month
- Applicable to all employees

## Recent Regulatory Updates (2025-2026)

> ⚠️ **New Rest Day Pay Rules (Effective April 2026):**
> MOM has announced revised rest-day pay calculations for shift workers on 12-hour rotations. Under the new rules, rest-day work at the employer's request will be calculated on the **12-hour shift rate** (not the 8-hour standard rate). This increases the cost of rest-day work by approximately **50%** for terminal operators.
>
> _This is the curveball element — participants should discover this during their compliance check._
