# AI Reskilling Case Studies — Industrial Organisations

> **Compiled for:** Flow Forward with AI — Capstone Exercise Reference
> **Purpose:** Provide context for designing Vopak's reskilling programme

---

## Case Study 1: Shell — "Digital Academy"

### Background

Shell launched its Digital Academy in 2022 to upskill 30,000+ employees in data analytics, AI, and digital collaboration tools. The programme targeted non-technical staff across operations, finance, and HR.

### Approach

- **Phase 1 (Months 1-3):** Awareness campaign — "Digital Fluency Week" events at every major site. Leadership testimonials. No mandatory training — purely optional.
- **Phase 2 (Months 4-8):** Role-specific micro-learning modules (15-30 min videos + quizzes). Completion rate target: 80%.
- **Phase 3 (Months 9-12):** "Digital Champions" programme — peer mentors nominated by line managers. Champions led local workshops.

### Results

| Metric                                 | Result                      |
| :------------------------------------- | :-------------------------- |
| Total employees trained                | 28,500 (95% of target)      |
| Completion rate (Phase 2)              | 73% (below 80% target)      |
| Digital Champions nominated            | 450 across 40 sites         |
| Employee satisfaction (post-survey)    | 4.1 / 5.0                   |
| Self-reported productivity improvement | 22% average (self-reported) |

### Lessons Learned

- **What worked:** Peer mentoring (Champions) was the #1 driver of sustained adoption. Top-down mandates without bottom-up Champions failed.
- **What didn't:** Long video modules had low completion. Shorter, task-specific "micro-challenges" worked better.
- **Key insight:** "People learn AI by _using_ AI on their real work, not by watching videos about AI."

## Case Study 2: Siemens Energy — "AI Ready" Programme

### Background

Siemens Energy launched "AI Ready" in 2023, targeting 15,000 employees in engineering, operations, and commercial functions. The programme was mandatory for all managers and optional for individual contributors.

### Approach

- **Assessment-first:** All participants completed a baseline AI skills assessment (1-5 scale across 5 competencies). This identified skill gaps by department.
- **Personalised learning paths:** Based on assessment, employees were assigned one of three tracks: Explorer (basic), Builder (intermediate), or Architect (advanced).
- **Works Council engagement:** Formal TIA submitted before programme launch. Works Council given seat on programme steering committee.
- **Integration with performance:** AI skills added to annual development objectives (not KPIs — a key Works Council requirement).

### Results

| Metric                           | Result                                      |
| :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------ |
| Baseline assessment completion   | 92%                                         |
| Track completion (6 months)      | Explorer: 88%, Builder: 71%, Architect: 54% |
| Manager AI confidence (pre/post) | 2.8 → 4.2 (out of 5.0)                      |
| Time saved (self-reported)       | Average 3.2 hours/week per participant      |
| Works Council concerns           | 2 raised, both resolved within 30 days      |

### Lessons Learned

- **What worked:** Starting with a skills assessment personalised the experience. Employees felt it was relevant, not generic.
- **What didn't:** Making the programme mandatory for managers but optional for ICs created a two-speed workforce. Many ICs who opted out fell behind.
- **Key insight:** "Works Council engagement upfront avoided delays later. Treating them as partners, not obstacles, was critical."

## Case Study 3: BASF — "Digital Fitness" for Plant Operators

### Background

BASF's chemical plants face similar challenges to Vopak: shift workers, safety-critical operations, and a workforce with varying digital literacy. In 2023, BASF piloted "Digital Fitness" at 5 plants targeting 2,000 shift operators.

### Approach

- **Mobile-first:** All training delivered via smartphone app (crucial for shift workers who aren't at desks)
- **Gamified:** Points, badges, leaderboard by shift team. Completion unlocked real rewards (extra break time, plant merchandise).
- **Safety-anchored:** Every AI example was tied to safety: "AI helped identify a pattern in incident reports that prevented an equipment failure" — not abstract productivity claims.
- **Union engagement:** Works Council equivalent (Betriebsrat) consulted from Day 1. Union representatives co-designed the training modules.

### Results

| Metric                                       | Result                                       |
| :------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------- |
| Completion rate                              | 91% (highest across BASF digital programmes) |
| Net Promoter Score                           | +67                                          |
| Safety-related AI suggestions from operators | 34 submissions in first 6 months             |
| Time to incident report completion           | Reduced 40%                                  |

### Lessons Learned

- **What worked:** Gamification drove engagement enormously. Shift teams competed, creating peer pressure to participate.
- **What didn't:** Desktop-based training modules had <30% completion among operators. Mobile was essential.
- **Key insight:** "For operational staff, the 'Day in the Life' approach — showing AI solving their actual problems — was 10x more effective than generic AI demos."

## Common Themes Across All Cases

1. **Start with assessment** — know where people are before designing training
2. **Peer mentoring > top-down mandates** — Champions/mentors drive sustained adoption
3. **Short, task-specific exercises > long courses** — people learn by doing, not watching
4. **Engage Works Council/unions early** — treat them as design partners
5. **Measure properly** — self-reported productivity gains are useful but should be supplemented with objective metrics (time to complete task, error rates)
6. **Mobile matters for operational staff** — don't assume everyone sits at a desk
