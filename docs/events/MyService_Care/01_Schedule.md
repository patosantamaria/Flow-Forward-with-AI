# AI Empowerment Journey: MyService Care Team

**Date:** February 11, 2026
**Session Duration:** 09:30 - 16:00
**Objective:** Transform from reactive "Ticketmasters" to proactive "AI-Augmented Consultants".

> **Core Philosophy:** Crawl (Trust), Walk (Collaborate), Run (Automate), Build (Solve).

---

## Block A: "CRAWL" - The Trust & The Hook (09:30 - 10:30)

**Goal:** Establish psychological safety and master the PTCF prompting framework.

### 09:30 - 09:50 (20 min): Welcome & The "Service Safety" Demo

- **Concept:** AI is not just a chatbot; it's a multimodal operational partner.
- **Script:** "Welcome to the future of MyService Support. We are moving from reactive firefighting to proactive intelligence."
- **Live Demo 1 (Docs):** Trainer uploads the `Streamliner_Functional_Spec.pdf`.
  - _Prompt:_ "Can you summarize the core Customs Validation rules? I'm new to the team." (Shows instant knowledge recall).
- **Live Demo 2 (Visual):** Trainer opens Gemini on mobile, points camera at a complex server rack or a printed P&ID diagram (or a mock image on screen).
  - _Prompt:_ "Explain this diagram to me like I'm a junior consultant. Where is the potential failure point?"

### 09:50 - 10:10 (20 min): The Mindset & PTCF Framework

- **Concept:** Treat AI as a "Junior Analyst", not a search engine.
- **Activity:** The "Bad Briefing" Game.
  - _Trainer:_ "I need an email." (Audience asks: To who? About what?).
  - _Result:_ The PTCF Framework (Persona, Task, Context, Format).
- **MyService Example:**
  - _Task:_ Draft a Service Notification.
  - _Context:_ P1 Incident, ATG Polling Failure, Europoort.
  - _Format:_ Professional, bulleted workarounds.

### 10:10 - 10:30 (20 min): Hands-On "First Win"

- **Activity:** Everyone opens Google Docs.
- **Prompt:** "Help me write a 'Shift Handover' email for the Singapore team. Include sections for P1 Incidents, Pending Changes, and Watchlist Items."
- _Goal:_ Everyone leaves this block with a usable template.

**10:30 - 10:45: Break (15 min)**

---

## Block B: "WALK" - The Workspace Lightning Round (10:45 - 12:00)

**Goal:** Manage the "Ticketmaster" noise using Gemini in Workspace.

### 10:45 - 11:30 (45 min): The "Ticketmaster Toolkit" Tour

- **Gmail (The Filter):** "Show me all emails from 'Martijn' about 'Budget' or 'SLA'." (Finding the needle in the haystack).
- **Drive (The Librarian):** "Catch me up on the `Release_18_Specs.pdf`." (Reading technical docs instantly).
- **Sheets (The Analyst):**
  - _Demo:_ Open `MyService_Incident_Log_Q3.csv`.
  - _Prompt:_ "Highlight rows where 'Business Time Left' is < 2 hours. Create a pivot table of 'Root Cause' by 'Assignment Group'."
- **Slides (The Reporter):** "Create a slide titled 'SLA Breach Analysis' with a chart placeholder."

### 11:30 - 12:00 (30 min): Discovery Challenge

- **Task:** Participants have 30 minutes to find one "Hidden Gem" in their own inbox/drive.
- _Challenge:_ Find an old "Root Cause Analysis" document and ask Gemini to "Summarize the 3 key lessons learned."

**12:00 - 13:00: Lunch (60 min)**

---

## Block C: "RUN" - Advanced Intelligence (13:00 - 14:15)

**Goal:** Deep diagnostics using NotebookLM and Custom Gems.

### 13:00 - 13:45 (45 min): NotebookLM Deep Dive (The "Customs Debugger")

- **Concept:** The "Private Room" for sensitive data. Zero hallucination risk.
- **Activity:** The "Streamliner" Investigation.
  1.  Everyone opens NotebookLM.
  2.  Upload `Streamliner_Error_Log_Dump.txt` and `Streamliner_Functional_Spec.pdf` (from Sandbox).
  3.  **Prompt:** "Analyze the error code 'ERR-902'. usage the Functional Spec. Is this a Bonded or Free stock issue? Guide me to the fix."
- _Outcome:_ Solving a complex P1 incident in 2 minutes instead of 2 hours.

### 13:45 - 14:15 (30 min): Introduction to Gems (The "Global SDM")

- **Concept:** The "AI Colleague" that remembers your rules.
- **Demo:** Creating the "Global SDM" Gem.
  - _Instructions:_ "You are the Service Delivery Manager. Tone: Stern, Factual. Always reference the SLA PDF."
  - _Test:_ "Draft an escalation for Incident 992. Response time was 4h, target 30m."

**14:15 - 14:30: Break (15 min)**

---

## Block D: "BUILD" - The Capstone Challenge (14:30 - 16:00)

**Goal:** The "Friday Afternoon Crisis". Integrated, high-pressure simulation.

### 14:30 - 15:30 (60 min): The "Crisis" Scenario (Individual & Group)

- **Scenario:** It is 16:30 on Friday. 50 tickets just landed. Streamliner is down. You need to fix it and handover.
- **Phase 1: Individual Triage (20 min)**
  - Use **Gemini in Sheets** to find the 3 tickets breaching SLA in `Incident_Log.csv`.
  - Use **NotebookLM** to diagnose the "Error 902" root cause.
- **Phase 2: The Escalation (20 min)**
  - Use your **"Global SDM" Gem** to draft the perfect vendor email citing the contract.
- **Phase 3: Group "Deep Research" (20 min)**
  - _The Twist:_ The Terminal Manager asks "Why does this keep happening?"
  - _Task:_ Use **Gemini (Web)** or **Deep Research** to investigate "Common causes of Customs API failures in European Ports" or "Trends in Port Automation latency".
  - _Synthesis:_ Combine your private findings (NotebookLM) with public insights (Gemini) into a final "Root Cause Report" in a shared Google Doc.

### 15:30 - 16:00 (30 min): Show & Tell (The "Solution")

- **Activity:** Each group presents their "Crisis Resolution Package":
  1.  The SLA Breach List (Screenshot).
  2.  The Root Cause Diagnosis (One sentence).
  3.  The Escalation Email (Text).
  4.  The "Deep Research" Insight (One paragraph).
- **Wrap Up:** Awarding the "AI Champion" for the best prompt/insight.
