# Legal — "The Contract Minefield" (Phase 3 Capstone)

---

## The Scenario

Vopak is finalising a new long-term terminal operating agreement with a strategic customer in a Southeast Asian jurisdiction. The commercial team has agreed heads of terms, but the customer's legal team has returned a heavily marked-up contract — 47 tracked changes across 32 pages — with an internal sign-off deadline in 5 business days.

The General Counsel needs a prioritised issue list by end of day, along with a negotiation strategy brief. Treasury has flagged that the payment clause restructuring proposed by the customer affects their FX hedging model. Procurement notes the take-or-pay volumes referenced in Annex B are inconsistent with the commercial term sheet.

---

## Your Mission (Search → Apply → Refine)

### 1. Search (15 min)

> **Prompt:** "Research the legal framework for long-term terminal operating agreements in [Southeast Asian jurisdiction]. What are the standard enforceability requirements for take-or-pay clauses? Are there any foreign ownership restrictions or local law requirements that affect the governing law and dispute resolution clause? Summarise with source citations."

### 2. Apply (30 min)

> **Prompt:** "Act as a Senior Vopak Legal Counsel. You have a terminal operating agreement with 47 customer-proposed amendments. Prioritise the issues into three categories: (1) Red — non-negotiable, Vopak will not accept; (2) Amber — negotiable with guardrails; (3) Green — acceptable as proposed. Focus on these clause types: liability and indemnification, force majeure, payment and set-off rights, governing law and arbitration, termination triggers. Present as a prioritised negotiation matrix: Clause | Customer Position | Vopak Position | Category | Recommended Response."

### 3. Refine (20 min)

> **Prompt:** "Draft the negotiation response email to the customer's legal team. Tone: professional, collaborative, solution-oriented. Acknowledge areas of agreement, introduce the Red issues as non-negotiable items with brief rationale, and propose a call to discuss the Amber items. Maximum 400 words. Do not reveal our internal categorisation."

---

## Cross-Department Check (at 60 min)

> "Loop in **Treasury** — confirm the payment restructuring impact on their FX hedging before accepting that clause. Check with **Commercial & BD** on the take-or-pay volume discrepancy in Annex B — we need the correct figures before the negotiation call. Flag the governing law clause to **IT/OT Digital** if it affects data residency for operational data flowing through this agreement."

2. Negotiation response email — ready to send after review
3. Open items list for cross-department resolution
