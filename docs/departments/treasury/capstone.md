# Treasury — "The Refinancing War Room" (Phase 3 Capstone)

---

## The Scenario

Vopak's Group Treasury team has been asked to prepare a refinancing proposal for a maturing EUR 500M revolving credit facility. The CFO needs a clear recommendation — renew with the existing bank group, syndicate to new lenders, or pursue a sustainability-linked structure — within 48 hours.

Two bank term sheets have arrived with conflicting pricing structures. Legal has flagged a covenant clause that may conflict with the existing Senior Notes. Procurement is asking whether the arrangement fee qualifies for early payment discount treatment.

---

## Your Mission (Search → Apply → Refine)

### 1. Search (15 min)

> **Prompt:** "Research the current European sustainable finance loan market. What are typical pricing structures for sustainability-linked loans (SLLs) for investment-grade infrastructure companies? What KPIs are banks typically linking to margin ratchets? Provide specific benchmark data and cite sources."

### 2. Apply (30 min)

> **Prompt:** "Act as a Vopak Treasury Analyst. You have two bank term sheets. Bank A offers: EUR 500M, EURIBOR + 130bps, 5-year tenor, no SLL component. Bank B offers: EUR 500M, EURIBOR + 120bps with a ±10bps SLL ratchet linked to GHG reduction and safety targets, 5-year tenor. Draft a side-by-side comparison table and a recommendation memo for the CFO. Include: all-in cost comparison, ESG alignment, covenant package differences, and refinancing risk assessment."

### 3. Refine (20 min)

> **Prompt:** "The Legal team flagged that Bank B's cross-default clause may conflict with clause 18.3 of our Senior Notes indenture. Draft a brief issue summary for the General Counsel explaining: what the conflict is, what waiver or amendment would be needed, and the likely timeline impact on the refinancing. Tone: precise, non-alarmist, solution-oriented."

---

## Cross-Department Check (at 60 min)

> "Coordinate with **Legal** on the covenant conflict. Check with **Procurement** on arrangement fee treatment. Loop in **Finance** to confirm the CAPEX pipeline — this affects the facility utilisation projections in the bank model."
