# Works Council Guidelines — Technology Introduction

> **Document:** Vopak Works Council — Framework for New Technology Introduction
> **Version:** 3.0 | **Effective:** March 2024
> **Scope:** All Vopak entities with Works Council representation

---

## 1. Purpose

These guidelines establish the process for introducing new technology that may affect working conditions, job content, employee privacy, or performance management. The Works Council has a statutory right to be consulted and, in specified cases, must provide formal non-objection before implementation.

---

## 2. When Consultation Is Required

### 2.1 Mandatory Consultation (Non-Objection Required)

The following changes **require formal Works Council non-objection**:

| Change Type                    | Examples                                                                                         |
| :----------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Performance monitoring**     | Any technology that tracks, records, or analyses individual employee performance or productivity |
| **Recruitment & selection**    | AI-assisted screening, automated candidate ranking, chatbot interviews                           |
| **Workforce restructuring**    | Role changes, headcount adjustments, or departmental reorganisation resulting from technology    |
| **Employee data processing**   | New systems that process personal employee data beyond existing HRIS                             |
| **Surveillance or monitoring** | Camera systems, keystroke logging, location tracking, email scanning                             |

### 2.2 Notification Only (No Non-Objection Required)

The following changes require **notification** but not formal non-objection:

| Change Type             | Examples                                                                                            |
| :---------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Productivity tools**  | New software that assists but does not monitor (e.g., AI writing assistant, spreadsheet automation) |
| **Training programmes** | New mandatory or optional training, including AI literacy programmes                                |
| **Communication tools** | New messaging or collaboration platforms                                                            |

---

## 3. The Consultation Process

### 3.1 Technology Impact Assessment (TIA)

Before introducing any technology requiring consultation, the requesting department must submit a **Technology Impact Assessment** containing:

1. **Description** of the technology and its intended use
2. **Impact on employees:** Which roles are affected? How does their work change?
3. **Data processing:** What employee data is collected, stored, or analysed?
4. **Privacy safeguards:** How is employee privacy protected?
5. **Human oversight:** What decisions remain with humans? Where does AI assist vs. decide?
6. **Reversibility:** Can the technology be rolled back if concerns arise?
7. **Training plan:** How will affected employees be prepared?

### 3.2 Timeline

| Step                           | Duration                     | Responsible                |
| :----------------------------- | :--------------------------- | :------------------------- |
| TIA submitted to Works Council | —                            | HR / Requesting Department |
| Works Council review period    | **30 calendar days**         | Works Council              |
| Questions and clarifications   | Within review period         | Both parties               |
| Works Council response         | By end of review period      | Works Council              |
| Implementation (if approved)   | After non-objection received | HR / IT                    |

### 3.3 Works Council Response Options

| Response                      | Meaning                                | Next Step                                  |
| :---------------------------- | :------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| **Non-objection**             | Works Council has no concerns          | Proceed with implementation                |
| **Conditional non-objection** | Approved with specific conditions      | Implement with stated conditions           |
| **Objection**                 | Works Council opposes the introduction | Escalate to mediation or withdraw proposal |
| **Request for extension**     | More time needed for review            | Extend review by up to 15 days             |

## 4. Specific Guidelines for AI in HR

### 4.1 AI in Recruitment

- **Permitted (with notification):** AI-assisted job description drafting, interview question generation, candidate communication drafting
- **Permitted (with consultation):** AI-assisted CV screening, automated skill matching
- **Prohibited:** AI as sole decision-maker for hiring, AI-generated candidate rankings without human review, use of AI to assess personality or cultural fit without candidate consent

### 4.2 AI in Performance Management

- **Permitted (with notification):** Using AI to draft development plans, summarise 360-feedback, identify training gaps
- **Permitted (with consultation):** AI-assisted performance analytics at team level (aggregated, anonymised)
- **Prohibited:** AI-generated individual performance scores, AI-driven promotion or termination recommendations, AI monitoring of individual productivity metrics

### 4.3 AI in Employee Communication

- **Permitted (with notification):** AI-assisted drafting of announcements, policy summaries, FAQ responses
- **No consultation required** for communication tools that assist drafting
- **Notification required** if AI-generated content is sent to employees without human review

---

## 5. Employee Rights

### 5.1 Right to Information

Employees have the right to know:

- When AI is used in any process that affects them
- What data about them is processed by AI systems
- How AI-assisted decisions can be challenged or reviewed

### 5.2 Right to Human Review

Any employee may request a **human review** of any AI-assisted decision that affects their employment, including: hiring, performance evaluation, promotion, training assignment, or disciplinary action.

### 5.3 Right to Opt Out

During the pilot phase of any new AI system, employees may **opt out** of AI-assisted processes and request the traditional human-only process. This right expires 6 months after the system is fully implemented and approved by the Works Council.
