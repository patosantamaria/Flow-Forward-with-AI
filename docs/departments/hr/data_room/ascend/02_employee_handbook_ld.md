## Section 20: Learning & Development Philosophy

Vopak is committed to the continuous development of its people. We believe that investing in our employees' growth is investing in the future of the company. Our L&D framework supports three pillars: **Technical Excellence**, **Leadership Development**, and **Digital & AI Literacy**.

---

## Section 21: Training Budget & Allocation

### 21.1 Annual Training Budget

Each Business Unit allocates a minimum of **2% of total payroll costs** to Learning & Development activities.

### 21.2 Individual Training Entitlement

- Each employee is entitled to **5 training days per year** (prorated for part-time)
- Training days include: classroom, e-learning, workshops, conferences, and coaching sessions
- Unused training days **do not carry over** to the following year

### 21.3 Budget Approval Process

| Training Cost     | Approval Required   |
| :---------------- | :------------------ |
| Up to EUR 500     | Line Manager        |
| EUR 500 - 2,000   | HR Business Partner |
| EUR 2,000 - 5,000 | BU HR Director      |
| Above EUR 5,000   | Regional VP HR      |

### 21.4 Mandatory Training

The following training is **mandatory** and does not count against the 5-day entitlement:

- Vopak Fundamentals on Safety (annual refresher)
- Code of Conduct (annual e-learning)
- Anti-Bribery & Corruption (annual e-learning)
- Data Privacy & Information Security (annual e-learning)
- AI Acceptable Use Policy (new — effective 2025, annual e-learning)

## Section 22: Certification & Professional Development

### 22.1 Certification Support

Vopak supports employees pursuing professional certifications relevant to their role:

- **Full cost coverage** for first attempt at approved certifications
- **Study leave:** up to 3 days for exam preparation (requires manager approval)
- **50% cost coverage** for second attempt; no coverage for third attempt

### 22.2 Approved Certification Programmes

Examples of approved certifications (not exhaustive):

- Safety: NEBOSH, IOSH, CSP
- Engineering: Chartered Engineer, PMP
- Finance: ACCA, CFA, CIMA
- HR: SHRM-SCP, CIPD
- Digital: Google Cloud Professional, AI certifications (Gemini, TensorFlow)

## Section 23: AI & Digital Skills Programme (New — 2025)

### 23.1 Flow Forward with AI

Vopak has launched the **Flow Forward with AI** programme to build AI literacy across the workforce. The programme has three phases:

| Phase   | Content                            | Duration | Target                |
| :------ | :--------------------------------- | :------- | :-------------------- |
| Phase 1 | AI Foundations & Ethics            | 1 day    | All knowledge workers |
| Phase 2 | Core AI Tools (Gemini, NotebookLM) | 1 day    | All knowledge workers |
| Phase 3 | Advanced: Gems, Automation, Agents | 1 day    | Selected AI Champions |

### 23.2 AI Champions Network

Each department must nominate at least **2 AI Champions** by end of Q4. Champions receive:

- Priority access to advanced AI tools and training
- Direct line to the Centre of Excellence (CoE)
- Recognition as "AI Pioneers" in internal communications
- Quarterly AI Champions summit (virtual)

### 23.3 Skills Assessment

All employees will complete an **AI Skills Self-Assessment** (see Appendix F) rating themselves 1-5 on:

1. AI awareness (what it is, what it can/can't do)
2. Prompt engineering (PTCF framework)
3. Tool proficiency (Gemini, NotebookLM, Workspace AI features)
4. Ethical AI awareness (bias, privacy, guardrails)
5. Applied AI (using AI for real work tasks daily)

## Section 24: Performance & Development Reviews

### 24.1 Annual Performance Cycle

| Activity                    | Timing            |
| :-------------------------- | :---------------- |
| Goal setting                | January           |
| Mid-year review             | June-July         |
| Year-end review             | November-December |
| Calibration                 | December          |
| Feedback & development plan | January           |

### 24.2 Use of Technology in Performance Management

- **Permitted:** Using AI to draft development plans, summarise multi-source feedback, identify training recommendations
- **Not permitted without Works Council approval:** Using AI to generate performance ratings, rank employees, or make promotion/termination recommendations
- **Never permitted:** Using AI as the sole basis for any adverse employment decision

### 24.3 Works Council Consultation

Any new technology or process that materially affects how employee performance is assessed, recorded, or used for employment decisions **requires formal Works Council consultation** before implementation. The HR team must:

1. Submit a Technology Impact Assessment (TIA) to the Works Council
2. Allow a **30-day consultation period**
3. Address all Works Council concerns in writing
4. Obtain formal Works Council non-objection before proceeding
