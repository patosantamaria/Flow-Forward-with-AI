# Vopak Employee Handbook — Extract: Shift Work & Compensation

> **Document Classification:** Internal — HR Use Only
> **Version:** 4.2 | **Effective Date:** 1 January 2025
> **Applicable to:** All Vopak Singapore Pte Ltd employees on shift rotation

---

## Section 7: Working Hours & Shift Patterns

### 7.1 Standard Working Hours

The standard working week for non-shift employees is **44 hours**, spread across 5.5 days (Monday to Saturday half-day). This complies with the Employment Act (Part IV).

### 7.2 Shift Patterns

Terminal operations employees may be assigned to one of the following shift patterns:

| Pattern | Description                           | Hours per Shift | Typical Application               |
| :------ | :------------------------------------ | :-------------- | :-------------------------------- |
| 5-2     | 5 days on, 2 days off                 | 8 hours         | Administrative, warehouse         |
| 4-3-3-4 | Alternating 4-on/3-off and 3-on/4-off | 12 hours        | Terminal operations, control room |
| 4-4     | 4 days on, 4 days off                 | 12 hours        | Jetty operations                  |

### 7.3 Night Shift Definition

A "night shift" is any shift that includes **at least 4 hours** between 23:00 and 07:00.

### 7.4 Maximum Consecutive Night Shifts

Employees shall not work more than **7 consecutive night shifts** without a minimum 24-hour rest period.

---

## Section 8: Shift Allowances

### 8.1 Shift Allowance Rate

Employees assigned to rotating shift patterns (7.2) are entitled to a **shift allowance of 15%** of their basic monthly salary.

### 8.2 Night Shift Premium

An additional **$25 per night shift** is payable for shifts meeting the night shift definition (7.3).

### 8.3 Public Holiday Premium

Employees required to work on a gazetted public holiday receive **200% of their daily rate** for hours worked.

### 8.4 Overtime Rate

Overtime is calculated at **1.5 times** the hourly basic rate for hours worked beyond the scheduled shift duration.

---

## Section 9: Overtime Limits

### 9.1 Monthly Overtime Cap

In accordance with the Employment Act (Part IV), **no employee shall work more than 72 hours of overtime per month**.

### 9.2 Daily Working Hour Limit

The total number of working hours (inclusive of overtime) **shall not exceed 12 hours in any one day**.

### 9.3 Overtime Approval

All overtime must be **pre-approved** by the Shift Superintendent or Terminal Manager. Unapproved overtime will not be compensated.

### 9.4 Record Keeping

The company maintains accurate records of all overtime worked. Records are retained for **2 years** as per MOM requirements.

---

## Section 10: Rest Days

### 10.1 Minimum Rest Days

Each shift employee is entitled to **1 rest day per week** without pay deduction. For the 4-3-3-4 pattern, rest days are built into the rotation cycle.

### 10.2 Work on Rest Day

If an employee agrees to work on a rest day:

- **At the employee's request:** Paid at 1x basic rate (for hours up to half the shift), 2x basic rate (for hours exceeding half the shift)
- **At the employer's request:** Paid at 2x basic rate for the full period worked

## Section 12: Onboarding & Probation

### 12.1 Probation Period

All new hires undergo a **6-month probation period**. During probation:

- Notice period is **1 week** (vs. 1 month for confirmed staff)
- Performance review at Month 3 and Month 6
- Full benefits (medical, dental) commence after **confirmation**

### 12.2 Safety Induction

All terminal operations employees must complete the following before their **first independent shift**:

1. Vopak Fundamentals on Safety induction (Day 1)
2. Site-specific safety orientation (Day 1-2)
3. IS-Safe tablet training (Day 2-3)
4. SHEQ induction with terminal SHEQ officer (Day 3-5)
5. Buddy shadowing: minimum **10 supervised shifts** before independent operation
6. Assessment sign-off by Shift Superintendent

### 12.3 Mandatory Certifications

Before working independently, terminal operators must hold:

- Valid Workplace Safety & Health (WSH) certification
- Relevant chemical handling certification for assigned product classes
- First aid certification (BCLS+AED preferred)
