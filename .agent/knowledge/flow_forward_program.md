# Flow Forward with AI — Program Bible

## Mission

**Transform how Vopak employees think about AI** — not just teach them tools. The program's success is measured by mindset shift, not feature adoption.

> "We're not training people to use a tool. We're helping them discover a new way of thinking."

## The Mindshift Model

### Before: The Tool Mindset

- "AI is a fancy search engine"
- "I need to learn a new interface"
- "This replaces what I already do"
- Fear of making mistakes, looking silly

### After: The Collaboration Mindset

- "AI is a capable colleague I can delegate to"
- "I explain what I need, like I would to a team member"
- "This augments my expertise — I bring judgment, AI brings speed"
- Curiosity, experimentation, iteration

### The Key Insight

**Prompting = Explaining to a Colleague.** When people stop thinking about "commands" and start thinking about "conversations," everything clicks. Ask yourself:

- What would I tell a new team member to get started on this task?
- What context would they need?
- What does "good" look like?

That's your prompt.

## The Gemini Personas — AI as Your Team

Each Workspace app has Gemini showing up as a different "colleague" with a distinct role. This analogy makes AI tangible and relatable:

| App               | Persona               | What They Do                                                             | Example                                                                    |
| ----------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Gmail**         | 📬 The Chief of Staff | Drafts responses, summarizes long threads, catches what you missed       | "Hey, can you write a polite follow-up to the team about the Q3 deadline?" |
| **Google Drive**  | 📚 The Librarian      | Finds documents, summarizes files, knows where everything is             | "Find me all the reports about terminal expansion from last quarter"       |
| **Google Docs**   | ✍️ The Co-Writer      | Drafts sections, rewrites for tone, expands bullet points into prose     | "Take these notes and turn them into a professional project update"        |
| **Google Sheets** | 📊 The Analyst        | Writes formulas, organizes data, spotts patterns                         | "Create a formula that calculates the average turnaround time by terminal" |
| **Google Slides** | 🎨 The Designer       | Generates slides, creates visuals, summarizes content into presentations | "Turn this quarterly report into a 5-slide executive summary"              |
| **Google Meet**   | 📝 The Note-Taker     | Captures meeting notes, action items, follow-ups                         | "What were the key decisions from today's meeting?"                        |

### How to Use This Analogy

When training, **introduce each app through its persona first:**

- "In Gmail, you have a Chief of Staff. What would you delegate to a real Chief of Staff? That's what Gemini does here."
- "In Drive, think of Gemini as the company librarian who has read every single document. Just ask."

This removes the "learning a new tool" barrier and replaces it with a familiar mental model.

## Change Management Framework

### The 3 Phases of Adoption

```
Phase 1: DISCOVER          Phase 2: PRACTICE         Phase 3: OWN
"That's interesting"    →  "Let me try that"      →  "I can't work without it"
Awareness & Curiosity      Hands-on Application      Habit Formation
Keynote, Demo             Workshop, Sandbox          Champions, Nudges
```

### Overcoming Resistance

| Resistance Type            | Root Cause             | Strategy                                                              |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "I'm too busy"             | Fear of learning curve | Show 2-minute wins (summarize email, draft reply)                     |
| "It's not accurate"        | Perfectionism          | Explain: AI gives 80% draft, you add 20% judgment                     |
| "It'll replace me"         | Job anxiety            | Reframe: "It replaces tasks, not people"                              |
| "I don't know what to ask" | Prompt anxiety         | Use the colleague analogy — "just explain like you would to a person" |
| "It's a toy"               | Skepticism             | Show ROI data from their own department                               |

### The 80/20 Rule of Adoption

- **80% of value** comes from **3 simple actions:** summarize, draft, find
- **Don't start with advanced features.** Start with "What's one task you'd love to delegate?"
- **Complexity comes naturally** once the mindset shifts

## Content Philosophy

### Structure content logically: Simple → Complex

1. **Start with "Why"** — Why does this matter for YOUR work?
2. **Show the simplest win** — A 10-second demonstration
3. **Build to complexity** — Layer features progressively
4. **End with ownership** — "Now create your own Gem for your daily workflow"

### The Colleague Test

Before publishing any training content, apply this test:

- "Could I explain this to a colleague over coffee in 2 minutes?"
- If not, simplify. Break it down. Use an analogy.
- Every concept should pass the "coffee test."
